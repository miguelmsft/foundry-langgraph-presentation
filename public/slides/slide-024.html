The Scenario

You manage a manufacturing facility with:

📦 25 parts in inventory
Pumps, motors, sensors, circuit boards, robotic arms
🏢 3 warehouses
Warehouse-A, B, and C
🚚 20 shipments
From 14 global suppliers
🌍 Global supply chain
Detroit, Tokyo, Shenzhen, Hamburg, São Paulo...

Built-in problems for the agent to find:

  • 🚨 9 parts below reorder point (critically low stock)
  • ⚠️ 3 shipments delivered late · 🔴 2 shipments currently delayed
  • 💰 Biggest shortage: P001 Hydraulic Pump — 38 units short = $93,100
The data is synthetic but realistic. We have 25 different parts across three warehouses with varying stock levels, costs, suppliers, and lead times. There are 20 shipments from 14 suppliers around the world. And we've deliberately planted problems: 9 parts below their reorder point, 3 late deliveries, 2 currently delayed shipments, and some parts with both low stock AND long lead times. The question is: can the agent find all of these autonomously?
