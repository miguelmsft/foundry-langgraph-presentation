Foundry gives you three agent types, each for different levels of control. Prompt Agents are the no-code option — you configure them entirely through the portal. Great for prototyping. Workflow Agents let you orchestrate multiple agents declaratively with YAML — think of them as agent pipelines. Both are fully managed. Then there are Hosted Agents — the topic of today's talk — which give you full code-level control. You write the code, pick your framework, and Foundry handles everything else: hosting, scaling, identity, telemetry.
